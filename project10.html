User-Centered Design

This user-centered design team project aimed to design a mobile application to solve a real world problem in a manner that incorporates joy. We decided to design an app to help students better manage their time so that they can have more time to spend on other things in life and therefore increase joy and self-care. Steps taken included research, design critiques, empathy mapping, contextual inquiries, lo-fi prototype, hi-fi prototype, and prototype refinements. We used tools such as Miro and Figma for collaboration and prototypes.
